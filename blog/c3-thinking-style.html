What is time?

Time, Dear Reader, Is Undefined.

2014-11-12

I always have a significant amount of skepticism when taking personality tests. They reek of over aggressive compression. For example, the learning styles test we took has 4 possible outcomes. This means that tries to compress the entirety of your consciousness, the whole learning, testing counterfactuals, observing reality, process into 2 bits of information. I mean, people who often chafe at the idea of being slotted down into a number will put an awful lot of stock into tests like these or say, the zodiac, which is a little less than 4 bits of information. They are slotting themselves into numbers, and exceptionally tiny ones at that!

With this in mind, I'm reflecting on how I felt over these past 2 and a half weeks compared with the result of being slotted into the "abstract sequential" camp. There were 14 statements it makes about AS folk, and by extension me. Of those 14, I'd say 9 apply to me fairly well and how I've felt so far, 4 are too ambiguous to reasonably come down yeah or nay. And 1 I felt like I flat out disagreed with. That's pretty good.

But what about the other 3? 16 statements for CS, 15 for CR and 16 for AR. Going over them I get 35 agreements, 8 disagreements, and 4 ambiguous! So I am more probably AS than any of them at random, but a lot of the statements seemed like kind of vacuous platitudes, like, "likes to maintain friendly relationships". People that dislike friends aren't a special learning category, they're misanthropes. That Said, I will respect the spirit of the exercise. The design work does fall under the category of sentimental thinking in my book, and I did chafe under it, coupled with the time constraints my poor planning left me with it was the most problematic of the 3 weeks. Of course with something this subjective finding causal linkages is a mugs game, but hey, those are the observations.

The dynamic-static dichotomy is very much part of why I find 2 bits (a single base-pair of DNA!) insufficient information to describe significantly how someone learns. How do you categorize someone with a mix between those 4 states? Knowing that Truth with a capital T is elusive and fleeting, and deciding it is all the more precious for that gets you a pretty solid leg up on learning. Anyone with a modest appreciation for history can see that things are learnable, and it comes down to how hard are they willing to hike, and how good of a guide do they have for the schlep up the mountain.
